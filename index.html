Conflict of Interest

Shot List
Written by Rosie Fellig & Anna Shohfi
Scene 1  |  INT. Dorm Room  |  Day
  • 01
    Wide
    Establishing
    Lauren's hyper-feminine dorm room fills the frame. Pink walls, neatly hung movie posters, stuffed animals piled on the bed, a throw blanket, desk buried under lip gloss, diaries, and a half-empty iced coffee. Lauren wears a cute, skimpy matching pajama set. Aiden and Lauren lay together on the bed. The camera opens tight on their intertwined hands before slowly pulling back to reveal the room.
    CameraStatic wide, slow pull-back from hands to room
    Lens24mm, low angle on bed
    LightingWarm golden afternoon sun through sheer curtains; soft, diffused fill
    SoundAmbient dorm noise; distant music from another room; quiet intimacy
    Mood / NotesEstablish Lauren's world first. The room should feel overwhelmingly, almost satirically feminine. The warmth here is a contrast to every setting that follows.
  • 02
    Extreme
    Close-Up
    Aiden plays with Lauren's hands, examining her bitten-down nails with exaggerated horror. She's embarrassed but laughing. He pulls her hand back with sudden force, then starts kissing her thumbs one by one, completely sincerely. She melts a little. He is effortlessly, infuriatingly charming.
    CameraExtreme close-up on hands; occasional rack focus to her face reacting
    Lens85mm macro
    LightingContinued warm afternoon gold; shallow depth of field, pink bedspread bokeh behind
    SoundHer quiet laugh. No music yet.
    Mood / NotesThis shot is about tenderness that he will never replicate again. The audience should want to stay here. Do not rush it.
    AIDEN There the sexiest thumbs I've ever seen. Let me see those nubbs.
  • 03
    Two-Shot
    Aiden's phone buzzes. He picks it up, rolling away slightly. Lauren stares at the ceiling, lost in her own thoughts. He tilts the phone toward her without looking away from it himself: a Goldman Sachs email, subject line "You're him. Let me be you?" She reads it, laughs at the absurdity. She asks if he wants to come to sorority date night Thursday. He gives a non-answer, eyes already back on the screen.
    CameraTwo-shot from foot of bed; she's foregrounded, he's angled away
    Lens35mm
    LightingSame warm room light; the phone screen adds a cold blue cast to his face only
    SoundNotification chime breaks the quiet. Subtle shift in score tone.
    Mood / NotesThe phone is the first intrusion. His body language has already left the room. Lauren hasn't noticed yet, or has chosen not to.
    LAUREN (reading aloud) "You're him. Let me be you?" AIDEN That's how you get them to answer. Subject line they can't scroll past.
  • 04
    Two-Shot
    Aiden puts his phone face-down, gets out of bed, and begins buttoning his shirt with practiced efficiency. Lauren sits up slowly, watching him. The energy shifts. She asks what this is. He turns away to avoid her eyes and delivers the Whiplash breakup monologue almost verbatim: "I'm not going to be the best version of myself with you." She catches it immediately. He kisses her hand once, holds it a half-second longer than he should, then walks out without looking back. The door closes quietly.
    CameraStarts two-shot; cuts to OTS on Lauren as he speaks; ends on her face alone as door closes
    Lens50mm
    LightingRoom light slightly cooler now. The afternoon has shifted. Shadow falls across Lauren.
    SoundScore drops out entirely when the door closes. Silence.
    Mood / NotesThe Whiplash reference is the joke and the wound simultaneously. He is not self-aware enough to be cruel on purpose, which makes it worse. Lauren's laugh at catching the reference should die fast.
    LAUREN Are you quoting the breakup scene from Whiplash right now? AIDEN We both are dedicated to our craft.
Scene 2  |  INT. Huntsman Hall, Waiting Room  |  Day
  • 05
    Medium Shot
    Hard cut to: a sterile corporate waiting room. Rows of identical chairs. Every guy here looks like Aiden. Aiden sits brimming with barely contained energy, already eyeing the guy next to him. He forces a conversation about extracurriculars. The guy is instantly uncomfortable. Aiden rattles off his resume like a grocery list, landing on "Trombone and Men for Menstruation Society" with enormous pride. The guy laughs awkwardly. Aiden elaborates: he sends cotton balls to Africa once every three months. The guy nods slowly and looks away.
    CameraMedium two-shot on the bench; Aiden slightly closer to camera to imply he's encroaching
    Lens35mm
    LightingHarsh fluorescent overhead; no warmth, no depth. Institutional.
    SoundAmbient HVAC hum. Muffled voices from offices. No score.
    Mood / NotesThe contrast with Scene 1 should feel jarring. This world is cold and performative. Aiden is in his element. He shouldn't be.
    AIDEN Trombone and Men for Menstruation Society. That Kathy lady ate it up.
  • 06
    Medium Shot
    A receptionist calls Aiden's name. He stands, turns to the guy, and initiates a dap. The guy doesn't clock it in time. Aiden waits. The guy slowly raises his hand. They try: Aiden's fist connects with his forearm. An awkward pause. They try again. Aiden punches him directly in the hand. The guy yelps. Aiden, unfazed, throws double finger guns at him and strides away.
    CameraMedium; holds wide enough to catch both of them through the full botched sequence
    Lens35mm
    LightingSame harsh fluorescent
    SoundThe yelp lands in dead silence. The finger guns are silent. Cut.
    Mood / NotesPlay this completely straight. No comic sting. Aiden's confidence is completely unshaken by the fact that he just punched a stranger. That is the joke.
    GUY Ow, fuck.
Scene 3  |  INT. Office Room  |  Day  |  The Interview
  • 07
    Medium Shot
    Aiden enters a generic office: conference table, scattered papers, framed Goldman poster on the wall. JARED (30s, clean-cut, khakis, the kind of guy who quotes Moneyball) stands and extends his hand with a warm smile. Aiden shakes it firmly, makes solid eye contact, recites his name back. For a moment, this feels like it's going to go well. Aiden allows himself to relax.
    CameraMedium; catches the handshake front-on; slight low angle on Jared to subtly establish power
    Lens50mm
    LightingNeutral office fluorescent with a warmer desk lamp in frame; deliberately unremarkable
    SoundA single low piano note begins. Almost imperceptible.
    Mood / NotesJared's warmth is genuine-seeming. Do not tip the hand yet. The audience, like Aiden, should feel cautiously optimistic.
    JARED Aiden, it's nice to meet you. Hopefully not giving you too much of a hard time?
  • 08
    Close-Up
    Reaction
    Aiden delivers his why-Goldman pitch with total commitment, landing on "Goldman is the gold standard" like a punchline he's proud of. Jared goes still. Then, slowly, he brings his hand up to his mouth. His eyes go wide. He mouths "oh my god" silently, over and over, staring just past Aiden. His expression is not impressed. It is barely contained contempt passing as amusement. Aiden clocks something is wrong but can't name it. He lets out an uncertain laugh.
    CameraTight close-up on Jared's face; cut to Aiden's slightly confused smile; back to Jared
    Lens85mm, flat
    LightingDesk lamp catches the shine in Jared's eyes. He looks clinical.
    SoundScore tension holds. No resolution.
    Mood / NotesJared's "oh my god" must read as mockery to the audience and ambiguous to Aiden. His line reading of "inspired, poignant" should be completely flat.
    JARED Wow. You chat that man? That's inspired, poignant.
  • 09
    Over-the-Shoulder
    The interview turns. Aiden answers a commitment question. Jared pauses, looks at his notepad, then without inflection asks: "Do you respect your mom?" Aiden says yes. "Do you like women?" A beat. Aiden says yes. "Sexually?" Longer beat. "Do you like brunettes?" Aiden opens his mouth. Nothing comes. He tries to redirect. Jared watches him, completely calm, pen still. The stillness is more unsettling than the questions.
    CameraOTS from behind Aiden looking at Jared; Jared fills the frame, Aiden's shoulder and ear visible
    Lens85mm compressed
    LightingJared is lit; Aiden's side is slightly cooler, slightly darker
    SoundScore drops out. Room noise only. The HVAC feels loud suddenly.
    Mood / NotesJared's power is in his stillness. He is never angry, never loud. Each question lands in the silence after the last one. Aiden's stammering is what carries the horror.
    JARED Do you like brunettes? AIDEN Ye... I'm sorry, how is this relevant?
  • 10
    Medium Shot
    Without warning, Jared stands. Pushes back from the table. Leans forward on his hands and looks down at Aiden with something between boredom and satisfaction. He does not extend his hand. He slides his business card across the table with one finger. Aiden stares at it. His face goes blank. He picks up the card, stands, and walks to the door. His hand is on the knob before Jared speaks.
    CameraMedium on Jared standing; insert on card sliding across desk; cut to Aiden's face; wide as he walks to door
    Lens35mm, slight high angle on Aiden
    LightingUnchanged. The mundanity of the room makes the humiliation worse.
    SoundCard sliding on table. Chair scrape. Silence.
    Mood / NotesThe card is the killing move. It replaces the handshake. Jared is not cruel out of malice; he is simply finished. That indifference is the worst thing he could do to someone like Aiden.
    JARED I think we're done here. What a waste of time this was. Have a good day, Aiden.
Scene 4  |  INT. Huntsman Hall, Waiting Room  |  Day  |  The Walk of Shame
  • 11
    Wide Shot
    Aiden walks out of the office hallway and back through the waiting room. He moves slowly, eyes down, tie slightly loosened. In the background, soft-focused: the guy from the bench is now standing, shaking hands with an interviewer, beaming. He got it. Aiden passes without seeing him. He looks down at the business card in his hand as he exits through the glass doors.
    CameraWide, static; Aiden walks toward camera while background action plays out behind him in focus
    Lens35mm; rack focus from background handshake to Aiden's face mid-walk
    LightingFluorescent. Cold. Unchanged from before.
    SoundThe background handshake is silent to us. We are inside Aiden's head. Only footsteps.
    Mood / NotesDo not editorialize. Just hold the wide. Let the audience do the math: the guy he punched just got the job Aiden wanted.
  • 12
    Insert
    Match Cut
    Extreme close-up on Jared's business card in Aiden's hand: JARED SHOHFI, Goldman Sachs. The name registers. MATCH CUT: the same framing, the same light, a door nameplate in a dorm hallway. LAUREN SHOHFI. Hold on it.
    CameraExtreme close-up, identical framing both sides of the cut
    Lens85mm macro, overhead
    LightingCard: cold office light. Nameplate: warm hallway light. Same frame, different world.
    SoundA single chord, sustained. The kind that doesn't resolve.
    Mood / NotesThis is the pivot of the film. The match cut is the reveal. Hold both images long enough for it to land fully before cutting to Scene 5.
Scene 5  |  INT. Dorm Room Hall / Lauren's Room  |  Evening  |  The Confrontation
  • 13
    Medium Shot
    Aiden knocks on Lauren's door with urgency. She swings it open in a different outfit, confused and faintly hopeful. He brushes past her into the room. She crosses her arms. He wheels on her: "Your brother works at Goldman?" She stares at him. "I've told you this a million times." He tells her what Jared asked: the mom, the women, the brunettes. She bursts out laughing. It's absurd and she finds it genuinely funny. Then her face closes. She remembers she's supposed to be angry at him.
    CameraMedium; starts on door, follows them into the room; stays mobile, handheld
    Lens35mm handheld
    LightingEvening warm gold from the window again, but dimmer. The pink room feels different now that she's angry in it.
    SoundHer laugh is the most alive sound in the film so far. Let it breathe before it dies.
    Mood / NotesLauren's laugh is key. She still likes him. That is why this scene works. The anger and the affection are fighting each other in real time.
    AIDEN Your brother works at Goldman? LAUREN I've told you this a million times.
  • 14
    Two-Shot
    Aiden steps toward her, reaches for her hands, says he doesn't care about Goldman anymore. She pulls back. Something shifts in her. She picks up her bag. And then she delivers it: calm, clear, controlled. The speech about labels. Why do you need Goldman? Why did you need Wharton? It keeps escalating. "Labels mean EVERYTHING." She lands each word. "And it's clear they mean more to you than real humans do." She hoists her bag and walks to the door.
    CameraTwo-shot shifts to single on Lauren as the speech builds; cut to Aiden only for his silence
    Lens50mm
    LightingEvening light has shifted. The room is dimmer. Only the lamp behind Lauren is lit; she's backlit, slightly silhouetted.
    SoundNo score. Her voice carries the whole shot.
    Mood / NotesShe is not yelling. That is important. The more controlled she is, the more it destroys him. Aiden's silence here is his only honest moment in the film.
    LAUREN Why do we need a label? Why do you need Goldman? Why did you need Wharton? Labels mean EVERYTHING. And it's clear they mean more to you than real humans do.
  • 15
    Wide Shot
    Lauren leaves. The door clicks shut quietly. Aiden stands alone in her pink room as the light dims around him. He doesn't move for a moment. Then he starts looking: at the stuffed animals, the posters, the books stacked on her desk. He picks one up and looks at the cover like he's reading it for the first time. He can't remember the last time he asked her what she was reading. Or anything about her, really. He sets the book down. The room holds him.
    CameraWide, static; Aiden small in the frame, surrounded by her things
    Lens24mm, slightly elevated; he should feel small in her world
    LightingNatural dusk through the window. No artificial light on. The pink gradually grays.
    SoundNear silence. Distant campus sounds. A single sustained note, very quiet.
    Mood / NotesThe widest, quietest shot in the film. His stillness is the closest thing to character growth we will see. Do not cut away too soon.
Scene 6  |  EXT. Locust Walk  |  Day  |  One Summer Later
  • 16
    Medium / Wide
    A title card: ONE SUMMER LATER. Locust Walk in full autumn. Aiden walks along the path, hands in his pockets, eyes scanning. He stops at a bulletin board covered in flyers and posters. One jumps out: a glossy printed flyer, "Penn's Most Accomplished Interns," with Lauren's photo on it, smiling. The list beneath her: McKinsey, Bain, BCG, Palantir, Goldman Sachs. He stares at it. Then Lauren steps out from behind the board, staple gun in hand, a stack of flyers under her arm. She sees him. He sees her. They stand there.
    CameraStarts medium on Aiden walking; pushes in as he stops; wide when Lauren appears
    Lens35mm
    LightingBright autumn day; dappled tree light; open and airy in contrast to every interior so far
    SoundCampus ambience. Footsteps. A beat of silence when they see each other.
    Mood / NotesLauren should look good. Poised, put-together, unbothered. The staple gun is a perfect prop. She was in the middle of something. He walked into her world again.
  • 17
    Two-Shot
    Stilted small talk. Lauren fills the silence easily: she spent the summer at Palantir, got the offer last October. Aiden almost visibly realizes she was with him when that email came through. He said nothing. She keeps her voice light. She asks what he did. He hedges. "Worked in legal." She tilts her head. "Was it your dad's firm in New Jersey?" He pivots hard: "confidential clientele, high-brow work." A beat. And then, somehow, he asks if she would refer him for next summer.
    CameraTwo-shot with physical distance between them; no warmth in the framing
    Lens50mm
    LightingOpen daylight; no shadows; nowhere to hide
    SoundAmbient. No score. The silences between lines do the work.
    Mood / NotesWhen he asks for the referral the camera should hold completely still. No reaction shot. Just the question hanging in the air. Make the audience feel it before Lauren does.
    LAUREN Was it your dad's firm in New Jersey? AIDEN I worked with a lot of, um, confidential and high-brow clients.
  • 18
    Wide Shot
    Final Frame
    Lauren looks at him for a long moment. Then: "Yeah, of course, Aiden. And while we're at it, I'll give you head." She unloads. Fuck Goldman. Fuck the non-profit. Fuck the period cotton balls. She walks. Calls back over her shoulder: "And I'm removing you on LinkedIn!" She is gone. Aiden stands on Locust Walk alone. He pulls out his phone. Opens LinkedIn. His face crumbles. He looks up from the screen, mouth open.
    CameraStarts two-shot; widens as she walks away; ends on Aiden alone in the wide frame, very small
    Lens24mm for the final frame; he should be nearly dwarfed by Locust Walk
    LightingSame bright open day. The world doesn't care.
    SoundHer footsteps fade. Campus noise continues. His last line lands in full ambient sound. Hard cut to black. No score.
    Mood / NotesHis last line has to land as both the funniest and saddest moment of the film simultaneously. The wide frame is essential: he is a small person in a large world he is desperately trying to rank himself in. Hold on the empty walkway after he exits. Then black.
    LAUREN And I'm removing you on LinkedIn! AIDEN Fuck. I just hit 500.
